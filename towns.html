Popular towns and villages to visit

Bantry

Nestled along West Cork's rugged coast, enchants with its maritime heritage and scenic splendor. The town unfolds against a backdrop of lush hills cascading toward Bantry Bay, boasting historic sites like Bantry House and vibrant markets celebrating local crafts and seafood. Its allure lies in the fusion of natural beauty and rich history.

Skibbereen

Skibbereen weaves together heritage and vibrancy. With echoes of history in its Famine-era sites, the town bustles with life through markets and cultural events. Surrounded by nature's beauty, it stands as a testament to the heart of Ireland's southwest, beckoning exploration and connection.

Clonakilty

A vibrant gem in West Cork, exudes charm with its colorful streets and lively spirit. Famous for its music, food, and warm hospitality, this town delights visitors with artisanal markets, historical sites like Michael Collins House, and the nearby Inchydoney Beach, encapsulating the essence of coastal Ireland's allure.

Glengarriff

Glengarriff captivates with its serene beauty. This picturesque village boasts lush woods embracing Bantry Bay, inviting exploration of Garinish Island's exotic gardens. Tranquil waters, forested mountains, and a vibrant village ambiance blend seamlessly, offering a tranquil escape amidst nature's grandeur.

Castletownbere

A coastal jewel in West Cork, embodies tranquility amid rugged beauty. Nestled by Bantry Bay's shores, it's a haven for seafood enthusiasts and maritime history aficionados. With panoramic vistas of the Berehaven Harbour and Bere Island, it captivates with its scenic charm and a welcoming, unhurried ambiance.
